## General

### What is Markdown?

Markdown is a lightweight way to format text using simple characters. For example, `**bold**` makes text **bold**, and `# Heading` creates a heading. In Bokuchi, you don't need to memorize the syntax — the [toolbar](/guides/markdown-toolbar/) lets you format text with buttons.

### Is Bokuchi free?

Yes. Bokuchi is free and open source.

### Does Bokuchi require an internet connection?

No. Bokuchi works completely offline. Your files are stored locally on your computer, and no data is sent over the network.

## Files

### Where are my files saved?

Files are saved wherever you choose. Bokuchi does not use a proprietary storage location or cloud service.

### Can I open Markdown files created in other editors?

Yes. Bokuchi supports `.md`, `.txt`, and `.markdown` files. Any standard Markdown file will work.

### Is there auto-save?

Yes. Auto-save is enabled by default and saves your file 3 seconds after you stop typing. You can turn it off in [Settings](/guides/settings/) > Advanced.

### Will my tabs be restored when I reopen the app?

Yes. All open tabs, their content, and your settings are automatically restored when you reopen Bokuchi.

## Appearance

### How do I change the theme?

Go to [Settings](/guides/settings/) > Appearance, or click the theme name in the status bar at the bottom of the window. See [Themes](/guides/themes/) for details.

### How do I change the language?

Go to [Settings](/guides/settings/) > Interface > Language. Bokuchi supports [14 languages](/reference/supported-languages/).

## Troubleshooting

### Windows: SmartScreen shows "Unknown publisher"

This is normal for apps not signed with a commercial code-signing certificate. When your browser warns about the download, click **Save** or **Keep**. If SmartScreen shows a confirmation dialog, click **Keep** to proceed. See the [installation guide](/getting-started/installation/) for detailed steps.

### Can I export to PDF?

Currently, Bokuchi supports [HTML export](/guides/export/). PDF export is planned for a future release.

## Features

### What are variables?

Variables let you define a value once (like an author name or date) and reuse it across your document with `{{variableName}}`. See [Variables](/guides/variables/) for full details.

### Can I paste tables from Excel or web pages?

Yes. Bokuchi can automatically convert HTML tables and CSV/TSV data into Markdown tables when you paste them. See [Table Conversion](/guides/table-conversion/).
